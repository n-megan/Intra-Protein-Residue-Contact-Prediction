Background:
Residue-residue interactions can be inferred via distance-based residue contacts.

A residue is considered in contact with another if the Euclidean distance between their atoms is within a defined threshold, based on the 3D structure of the protein (expressed as the x, y, and z coordinates of the amino acids' atoms).

This tool determines contacts based on the alpha-carbon (Cα) and beta-carbon (Cβ) atoms of different residues, where the distance cutoff is 5 Å.

User Guide:
(1) Enter a UniProt or PDB ID. If you enter a PDB ID, you may be asked to select a UniProt ID. A short description will be displayed before UniProt ID selection.

(2) The predicted residue-residue interactions for alpha-carbon and alpha-beta atoms of residues will be displayed.

(3) A contact map is included to help visualize the distance between residue pairs of the 3D protein structure.

Protein information is retrieved from AlphaFold and UniProt.
